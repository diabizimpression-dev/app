What Is GLP-1?

A hormone your body already makes — now working for you.

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) is a naturally occurring hormone that tells your brain you're full, slows digestion, and helps regulate blood sugar. Prescription GLP-1 medications boost that signal so cravings quiet down, portions feel right, and weight comes off steadily.

FAQ

Your most common GLP-1 questions

Am I a good candidate for GLP-1?

Most adults with a BMI of 27 or higher, or 25+ with weight-related health goals, are eligible. Your physician will confirm during your review. GLP-1 is not recommended during pregnancy or with certain thyroid conditions.

What are the side effects?

The most common are mild nausea, constipation, or fatigue during the first few weeks as your body adjusts. Your care team helps you dose carefully to minimize these, and most patients find them fade within a month.

Do I need insurance?

No. MEDVi's program is paid monthly out of pocket — no insurance, no surprise billing, and you can cancel anytime.

How fast will I see results?

Most patients feel appetite changes within the first week and see the scale move by week 3–4. The larger 15–20% loss typically lands between months 6 and 12.

Is the medication FDA-approved?

Compounded GLP-1s are produced in FDA-regulated facilities. Although these facilities are highly regulated, the compounded medications themselves are not FDA-approved or evaluated for safety, efficacy, or quality. Your clinician will discuss the options with you.

What if I stop the medication?

Our care team builds a long-term plan including nutrition, movement, and maintenance protocols so results stick. If you taper off, we stay with you through that transition too.
